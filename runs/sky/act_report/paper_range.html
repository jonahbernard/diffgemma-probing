paper: per-channel range

magenta/red = mxfp4 (W4A4), green = bf16. Outliers inflate fp4 block scales, so big per-block absmax = more quantization error.
What am I looking at?
Outlier-Suppression+ analog. OS+ shows that outlier channels have wide, asymmetric value ranges (e.g. one channel spans -97 to +43 while most span +/-3), which motivates shifting each channel to recenter it before quantizing. For one representative mid-network layer and GEMM site, each x-position is a channel (sorted by spread); the shaded band is roughly that channel's value range, centered on its mean (ch_mean) with extent set by its absmax. A few channels with bands far wider than the bulk -- and not centered on zero -- are the OS+ targets. green = bf16, red = mxfp4.

Each band ~ one channel's value range (mean +/- absmax), sorted by spread. Wide off-center bands = outlier channels OS+ targets. green = bf16, red = mxfp4.
